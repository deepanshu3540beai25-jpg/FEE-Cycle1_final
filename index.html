The Best Homemade Lasagna

Prep Time
30 min
Cook Time
50 min
Total Time
1 hr 20 min
Servings
8
Difficulty
Medium

Layers of rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and perfectly cooked pasta come together in this ultimate comfort dish. This is the lasagna you'll make for every special occasion — and it never disappoints.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna sheets (dried or fresh)
  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef + 200g (7 oz) ground pork
  • 1 large onion, finely diced · 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 800g (28 oz) crushed tomatoes · 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano · 1 tsp dried basil · salt & pepper
  • Béchamel: 60g butter, 60g flour, 700ml whole milk, pinch of nutmeg
  • 250g (9 oz) mozzarella, shredded · 80g (3 oz) Parmesan, grated

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium-high. Brown beef and pork, breaking it up, about 8 minutes.
  2. Add onion and garlic, cook 3 minutes. Stir in tomato paste and cook 1 minute.
  3. Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt and pepper. Simmer uncovered 20 minutes until thickened.
  4. Make béchamel: melt butter, whisk in flour 1 minute, gradually add milk whisking constantly until thick and smooth. Season with nutmeg, salt and pepper.
  5. Preheat oven to 180°C / 350°F. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the base of a 33×22cm baking dish.
  6. Layer: lasagna sheets → meat sauce → béchamel → mozzarella. Repeat 3 times. Finish with béchamel and Parmesan on top.
  7. Cover with foil and bake 30 minutes. Remove foil, bake another 20 minutes until golden and bubbling.
  8. Rest 10 minutes before cutting and serving.
💡 Pro Tip: Make the meat sauce a day ahead — the flavour deepens overnight and makes assembly much faster.

Storage & Make Ahead

Leftovers keep in the fridge for up to 4 days. Freeze tightly wrapped for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Rich & Hearty Beef Stew

Prep Time
20 min
Cook Time
2 hr 10 min
Total Time
2 hr 30 min
Servings
6
Difficulty
Easy

Tender chunks of beef slow-cooked in a deep, wine-enriched broth with hearty vegetables. This is the kind of stew that fills your kitchen with the most incredible aroma and warms you from the inside out.

Ingredients

  • 1.2kg (2.5 lb) beef chuck, cut into 4cm cubes
  • 3 tbsp plain flour · 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped · 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, chunked · 3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 400ml red wine · 600ml beef stock · 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 400g potatoes, quartered · 2 sprigs thyme · 2 bay leaves · salt & pepper

Instructions

  1. Pat beef dry, toss in seasoned flour. Brown in batches over high heat, 3 min per side. Set aside.
  2. Sauté onion and garlic 3 minutes. Add carrots and celery, cook 2 minutes.
  3. Add wine, scrape up browned bits. Reduce by half, about 4 minutes.
  4. Add stock, tomato paste, thyme, bay leaves and beef. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce to low, cover and simmer 1.5 hours until beef is almost tender.
  6. Add potatoes, cook uncovered 30 minutes. Remove herbs and serve.
💡 Pro Tip: Brown the beef in batches without crowding — this creates deep caramelised flavour instead of steaming.

Storage & Make Ahead

Keeps in the fridge 4–5 days and tastes even better the next day. Freeze up to 3 months.

Authentic Pad Thai

Prep Time
10 min
Cook Time
10 min
Total Time
20 min
Servings
2
Difficulty
Easy

Smoky, tangy, sweet and a little spicy — real Pad Thai made with tamarind paste and fish sauce in under 20 minutes. Skip the takeout; this is the real deal.

Ingredients

  • 180g dried rice noodles (3–5mm wide)
  • 200g shrimp or chicken, sliced · 2 eggs
  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste · 2 tbsp fish sauce · 1 tbsp oyster sauce · 2 tsp sugar
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil · 3 garlic cloves, minced · 2 shallots, sliced
  • 100g bean sprouts · 3 spring onions, chopped
  • To serve: crushed peanuts, lime wedges, chilli flakes

Instructions

  1. Soak noodles in room temperature water 30 minutes. Drain.
  2. Mix tamarind, fish sauce, oyster sauce and sugar. Set aside.
  3. Heat wok over very high heat until smoking. Stir-fry garlic and shallots 30 seconds.
  4. Add protein, stir-fry 2 minutes. Add noodles and sauce, toss vigorously 1 minute.
  5. Push to the side, scramble eggs, fold through noodles.
  6. Add bean sprouts and spring onions, toss 30 seconds. Serve immediately with toppings.
💡 Pro Tip: A screaming hot wok is everything — without it you get soggy steamed noodles, not smoky wok-fried perfection.

Storage & Make Ahead

Best eaten fresh. Leftovers keep 2 days. Reheat in a hot pan with a splash of water.

Creamy Butter Chicken

Prep Time
15 min
Cook Time
20 min
Total Time
35 min
Servings
4
Difficulty
Easy

Velvety tomato-cream sauce hugging tender marinated chicken. This is the butter chicken that converts even the most reluctant curry sceptics — rich, fragrant, and irresistible.

Ingredients

  • 700g chicken thighs, bite-sized pieces
  • Marinade: 3 tbsp yoghurt, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tsp each cumin/garam masala/turmeric/chilli, salt
  • 3 tbsp butter · 1 large onion · 5 garlic cloves · 1 tbsp ginger
  • 1.5 tsp garam masala · 1 tsp cumin · 1 tsp coriander
  • 400g crushed tomatoes · 1 tbsp sugar · 150ml heavy cream

Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken at least 20 minutes. Cook in hot pan until charred. Set aside.
  2. Cook onion in butter 5 minutes until golden. Add garlic, ginger and spices, cook 2 minutes.
  3. Add tomatoes and sugar, simmer 10 minutes. Blend until smooth.
  4. Stir in cream and chicken, simmer 5 minutes. Serve with rice and naan.
💡 Pro Tip: Blending the sauce is the secret to that silky, restaurant-quality texture. Don't skip it.

Storage & Make Ahead

Fridge 4 days. Freeze 3 months. Flavour deepens overnight — great for meal prep.

Cheesy Garlic Bread

Prep Time
5 min
Cook Time
10 min
Total Time
15 min
Servings
6
Difficulty
Easy

Golden, crispy, loaded with garlic butter and melted cheese. The side dish that steals the show every single time — nobody can ever eat just one slice.

Ingredients

  • 1 large baguette or Italian loaf
  • 120g unsalted butter, softened
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced · 3 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped · salt & pepper
  • 150g mozzarella, shredded · 50g Parmesan, grated

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C / 400°F. Mix butter, garlic, parsley, salt and pepper.
  2. Halve baguette lengthways. Spread butter generously. Top with both cheeses.
  3. Bake 8–10 minutes until golden. Grill 1–2 minutes for extra colour. Slice and serve.
💡 Pro Tip: Softened (not melted) butter gives you even garlic flavour in every bite — it bakes into the bread rather than pooling.

Storage & Make Ahead

Prep up to 24 hours ahead, refrigerate unbaked. Re-crisp leftovers at 180°C for 5 minutes.

Molten Chocolate Lava Cakes

Prep Time
10 min
Cook Time
12 min
Total Time
25 min
Servings
4
Difficulty
Medium

A perfectly set chocolate shell giving way to a river of warm, gooey molten centre. Incredibly impressive and ready in just 25 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 200g dark chocolate (70%), chopped · 120g unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 3 whole eggs + 3 egg yolks · 100g caster sugar
  • 40g plain flour · pinch of salt · cocoa powder for dusting
  • Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream to serve

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C / 425°F. Butter 4 ramekins and dust with cocoa powder.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter together. Stir until smooth. Cool slightly.
  3. Whisk eggs, yolks and sugar until pale and slightly thickened.
  4. Fold chocolate into egg mixture. Sift in flour and salt, fold until just combined.
  5. Divide into ramekins. Bake 10–12 minutes — edges set, centre wobbles.
  6. Rest 1 minute, run a knife around the edge and invert onto plates. Serve immediately.
💡 Pro Tip: Do a test run with one ramekin before your dinner party — every oven varies by 2–3 minutes.

Storage & Make Ahead

Fill ramekins and refrigerate up to 24 hours. Add 2 extra minutes if baking from cold.

Better-Than-Takeout Fried Rice

Prep Time
5 min
Cook Time
10 min
Total Time
15 min
Servings
3
Difficulty
Easy

Smoky, savoury fried rice with perfectly separated grains and that signature wok-breath flavour. The weekly fridge-cleaner you'll make forever.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups day-old cooked rice (cold from fridge)
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten · 1 cup frozen peas and corn · 3 spring onions, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced · 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce · 1 tbsp oyster sauce · 1 tsp sesame oil · ½ tsp white pepper
  • Optional: 150g diced protein (pork, shrimp, chicken)

Instructions

  1. Mix all sauces together in a small bowl.
  2. Heat wok over very high heat until smoking. Stir-fry protein if using, set aside.
  3. Add oil and garlic, stir-fry 20 seconds. Add cold rice, toss vigorously 2 minutes.
  4. Push rice aside, scramble eggs until just set, fold through rice.
  5. Add vegetables and protein, toss 1 minute. Add sauce, toss 1 minute. Serve.
💡 Pro Tip: Day-old cold rice is non-negotiable — spread on a tray and refrigerate overnight for the best results.

Storage & Make Ahead

Fridge 3 days. Freeze 1 month. Reheat in hot pan with a splash of water.

Crispy Beef Tacos

Prep Time
10 min
Cook Time
15 min
Total Time
25 min
Servings
4
Difficulty
Easy

Boldly seasoned ground beef in crispy shells piled high with fresh toppings. Fast, fun, and absolutely loaded with flavour — Taco Tuesday just got a serious upgrade.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef · 1 small onion, diced · 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • Seasoning: 1.5 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp paprika, ½ tsp oregano, salt & pepper
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste · 80ml water · 8–12 hard taco shells
  • Toppings: shredded lettuce, diced tomato, cheddar, sour cream, guacamole, jalapeños, lime

Instructions

  1. Brown ground beef over high heat until no pink remains, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add onion and garlic, cook 2 minutes. Add seasoning, cook 1 minute.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and water, simmer 3 minutes until sauce coats the beef.
  4. Warm shells at 180°C, fill with beef, add toppings, squeeze lime and serve.
💡 Pro Tip: A tiny pinch of cinnamon in the seasoning adds incredible depth you can't quite put your finger on.

Storage & Make Ahead

Beef filling keeps 4 days in fridge, freezes 3 months. Store toppings separately.

Creamy Mushroom Pasta

Prep Time
5 min
Cook Time
15 min
Total Time
20 min
Servings
2
Difficulty
Easy

Golden garlicky mushrooms in a silky Parmesan cream sauce tossed through perfectly cooked pasta. This 20-minute vegetarian pasta tastes like a proper Italian restaurant.

Ingredients

  • 200g fettuccine · 350g mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, oyster), sliced
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter · 4 garlic cloves, minced · 1 small shallot, diced
  • 150ml heavy cream · 50ml dry white wine · 60g Parmesan, grated
  • Fresh thyme · salt, pepper, chilli flakes · fresh parsley to serve

Instructions

  1. Cook pasta in salted water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining.
  2. Brown mushrooms in butter in a single layer, 2 minutes undisturbed. Toss, cook 2 more minutes. Season and set aside.
  3. Cook shallot and garlic 2 minutes. Add wine, reduce 1 minute. Add cream and thyme, simmer 2 minutes.
  4. Stir in Parmesan. Add mushrooms and pasta, toss with pasta water until silky. Serve.
💡 Pro Tip: Single layer, no stirring = golden caramelised mushrooms. Crowding = steamed and pale.

Storage & Make Ahead

Best eaten fresh. Reheat with a splash of cream. Mushrooms can be cooked 2 days ahead.

Mango Lassi

Prep Time
5 min
Total Time
5 min
Serves
2 glasses
Difficulty
Very Easy

Thick, creamy and gloriously golden — this mango lassi is the ultimate Indian refresher. Sweet ripe mango meets tangy yoghurt in a glass that feels like a mini holiday every single sip.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe mangoes (about 400g pulp) or 400g tinned Alphonso mango pulp
  • 200g full-fat plain yoghurt (or thick Greek yoghurt)
  • 100ml whole milk (adjust for consistency)
  • 2 tbsp sugar or honey, to taste
  • 4–5 ice cubes · pinch of cardamom powder
  • To garnish: dried rose petals, sliver of mango, pinch of cardamom

Instructions

  1. If using fresh mango, peel and roughly chop the flesh. Discard the stone.
  2. Add mango, yoghurt, milk, sugar and ice to a blender.
  3. Blend on high for 45–60 seconds until completely smooth and frothy.
  4. Taste and adjust sweetness. Add more milk if you prefer a thinner consistency.
  5. Pour into chilled tall glasses. Top with a pinch of cardamom and garnish. Serve immediately.
🥭 Pro Tip: Alphonso mango pulp (tinned) is the secret weapon — sweeter and more fragrant than most fresh supermarket mangoes, and available year-round.

Variations

For a richer lassi, replace milk with more yoghurt. For a vegan version, use coconut yoghurt and oat milk. A pinch of saffron steeped in 1 tbsp warm milk adds incredible depth.

Storage

Best consumed immediately. Can be refrigerated up to 24 hours — stir well before serving.

Masala Chai

Prep Time
2 min
Brew Time
8 min
Total Time
10 min
Serves
2 cups
Difficulty
Easy

Warming, aromatic and deeply comforting — proper masala chai brewed on the stovetop with whole spices, strong black tea and creamy milk. Exactly how it's made in Indian homes every morning.

Ingredients

  • 300ml water · 300ml whole milk
  • 2 tsp strong black tea leaves (Assam or CTC preferred)
  • 2 tbsp sugar (or to taste)
  • Whole Spices: 4 green cardamom pods (lightly crushed), 2 cloves, 1 small cinnamon stick, 1 thin slice fresh ginger, 2 black peppercorns
  • Optional: pinch of nutmeg, 1 star anise

Instructions

  1. Add water to a small saucepan over medium heat. Add all whole spices and ginger.
  2. Bring to a gentle boil and simmer 3 minutes to release the spice flavours.
  3. Add tea leaves and simmer 1 minute, stirring occasionally.
  4. Pour in the milk and bring back to a boil, stirring frequently to prevent sticking.
  5. Once it rises to a boil, reduce heat and simmer 2 minutes. Add sugar, stir to dissolve.
  6. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into cups. Serve steaming hot.
☕ Pro Tip: Let it boil up and come back down twice — this traditional technique gives chai its distinct layered flavour and characteristic frothy top.

Spice Notes

Start with the base spices (cardamom, ginger, clove) and build from there. Too much cinnamon can make it bitter. More milk = creamier and milder; less milk = stronger brew.

Storage

Best made fresh. Refrigerate brewed chai up to 2 days and reheat gently on the stovetop — do not microwave.

Sparkling Limonata

Prep Time
5 min
Total Time
8 min
Serves
4 glasses
Difficulty
Very Easy

Bright, fizzy and perfectly balanced between tart and sweet — this Italian-style sparkling lemonade is everything a summer drink should be. Made with fresh lemon juice and a simple herb syrup.

Ingredients

  • 150ml freshly squeezed lemon juice (about 5–6 lemons)
  • 500ml sparkling water, chilled · plenty of ice
  • Simple Syrup: 100g sugar, 100ml water, 4 sprigs fresh mint or basil
  • To garnish: lemon slices, fresh mint, edible flowers (optional)

Instructions

  1. Make the syrup: combine sugar and water in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir until dissolved, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat, add mint/basil, steep 5 minutes. Strain and cool completely.
  2. In a large jug, combine lemon juice and cooled syrup. Stir well.
  3. Fill glasses generously with ice. Pour lemon mixture halfway, top with sparkling water.
  4. Stir gently just once to preserve bubbles. Garnish and serve immediately.
🍋 Pro Tip: Roll the lemons firmly on the bench before squeezing — this breaks down the inner membranes and almost doubles the juice you get out.

Variations

Add fresh raspberries to the syrup for a berry limonata. For an adult version, replace half the sparkling water with Prosecco. Rosemary in the syrup instead of mint gives an elegant herbal note.

Storage

The lemon-syrup base keeps in the fridge up to 5 days. Only add sparkling water just before serving.

Matcha Rose Latte

Prep Time
3 min
Make Time
4 min
Total Time
7 min
Serves
1 glass
Difficulty
Easy

Earthy ceremonial-grade matcha meets the gentle floral sweetness of rose — poured over ice or steamed milk for one of the most beautiful drinks you can make at home. Café-worthy in under 10 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1½ tsp ceremonial-grade matcha powder
  • 2 tbsp hot water (80°C — not boiling)
  • 250ml oat milk or whole milk
  • 1–2 tsp rose syrup (or 1 tsp rose water + 1 tsp sugar)
  • Ice (for iced version)
  • To garnish: dried rose petals, dusting of matcha

Instructions — Iced Version

  1. Sift matcha into a small bowl. Add 2 tbsp of 80°C water.
  2. Whisk vigorously in a W-motion until smooth, frothy and no lumps remain, about 30–45 seconds.
  3. Stir in rose syrup.
  4. Fill a glass to the brim with ice. Pour cold milk over ice.
  5. Slowly pour the matcha mixture over the back of a spoon for a beautiful layered effect.
  6. Garnish with dried rose petals and a dusting of matcha. Stir before drinking.

Instructions — Hot Version

  1. Whisk matcha and hot water as above. Stir in rose syrup.
  2. Steam or heat milk until hot and frothy.
  3. Pour matcha into your cup, then slowly pour over steamed milk. Garnish and serve.
🍵 Pro Tip: Never use boiling water for matcha — above 85°C it turns bitter and destroys the amino acids that give it that signature umami sweetness.

Storage

Best made fresh. The matcha-rose concentrate (before adding milk) can be kept in the fridge up to 12 hours.
